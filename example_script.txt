TITLE: The Boring Money Habit That Quietly Makes People Rich

[VISUAL: slow aerial drone shot of a city skyline at sunrise, cinematic]
Most people think getting rich takes luck, a big salary, or perfect timing. [PAUSE] It doesn't. It takes one boring habit that almost nobody uses on purpose.

[VISUAL: close up of gold coins stacking on a wooden table, shallow depth of field]
That habit is compound interest. Albert Einstein supposedly called it the eighth wonder of the world. [PAUSE] Whether he said it or not, the math is undeniable.

[VISUAL: animated line graph curving sharply upward over time, clean minimal style]
A single dollar growing at ten percent a year doesn't just add up. It multiplies. After forty years, that one dollar becomes more than forty-five.

[VISUAL: a young person putting a coin into a glass jar, warm morning light]
The catch is time. The earlier you start, the less you actually have to save. [PAUSE] Someone who starts at twenty-five can invest half as much as someone who starts at thirty-five, and still end up with more.

[VISUAL: calm person walking through a quiet park, hands in pockets, content]
So the real secret isn't picking the perfect stock. It's starting early, staying consistent, and letting time do the heavy lifting. The boring path is the one that works.
